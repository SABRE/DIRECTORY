ATTRACTIONS:
Delight in Duncan’s hospitality and enjoy Oklahoma
history at its best! Experience the vibrant spirit of the
Chisholm Trail and discover the role it played turning
cowboys into legends, cattlemen into men of fortune,
and oilmen into industrial giants.
Discover the
Chisholm Trail Heritage Center
and see the largest bronze statue dedicated to the
Chisholm Trail in the world! Live the adventure of the
Old Chisholm Trail in the “hands on” youth gallery,
“meet” Jesse Chisholm for whom the Trail is named,
and Tex, a young Texas Cowboy, in the Campfire
Theater. Experience the multi-sensory Chisholm
Trail Experience Theater where you will see, hear,
feel, and smell the adventures cowboys experienced
along the Old Trail. Additional dioramas, displays and
the Garis Gallery of the American West make this an
unforgettable experience!
1000 Chisholm Trail Parkway, 580-252-6692
Visit the
Stephens County Historical Museum
featuring displays and artifacts illustrating the land run
and settlement life in Oklahoma alongside early 20th
century memorabilia. Located in the National Guard
Armory and placed on the National Register of Historic
Places, it truly is a step back in time!
Fuqua Park, intersection of Hwy 81 and Beech Ave,
580-252-0717
Also located within Fuqua Park is the
Rock Island
905 Historical Society.
Duncan saw the arrival of the
first scheduled Passenger train in 1892. In the early
days, the town prospered with the help of the Rock
Island Railroad, as did our great state of Oklahoma.
The 905 Steam Locomotive has been a symbol of that
heritage since it was first driven down Oak Street and
placed in Fuqua Park in 1954. It has been enjoyed
by many a resident and visitor, both young and old.
The Rock Island 905 Museum & Gift Shop is open
Tuesday through Saturday from 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM.
Fuqua Park, intersection of Hwy 81 and Beech Ave -
